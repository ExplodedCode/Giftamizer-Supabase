Child-list claim visibility

A review of the RLS change that lets a parent see and set claim status on their own "Display Separately" (child) lists, without opening a way to see or overwrite claims on anyone else's list — including their own.

ResultThe owner of a child list can now see who's claimed what and make the first claim on their own child-list items. They can never see or touch claims on a regular list, and can never overwrite a claim someone else already made — even on their own child's list.
Why it took 4 passesThe first version only opened SELECT + UPDATE. Testing surfaced two real gaps: claiming actually needs INSERT too, and granting UPDATE by ownership (rather than by who holds the claim) let an owner silently steal another member's claim. Both are fixed in the final file.

The request

"It would be helpful to see what people are getting the kids, sometimes I want to remove an item and give the idea to someone else. But I don't know if someone is already getting it for them." Original feedback · prompted the RLS change

Translated into policy: item status is hidden from an item's owner by design — that's what keeps your own gift claims a surprise. But on a child list, the "owner" isn't the recipient, so that surprise-protection was just getting in the way.

The core gate

is_child_list_item(itemid, userid)

select exists (
  select 1
  from items i
  join items_lists il on il.item_id = i.id
  join lists l on l.id = il.list_id and l.user_id = il.user_id
  where i.id = itemid
    and i.user_id = userid   -- must actually own the item
    and l.user_id = userid   -- must actually own the list
    and l.child_list = true
);

Both ownership checks matter: without i.user_id = userid, anyone able to link an arbitrary item into their own child list via items_lists could have used this as a side door into someone else's claim data.

Not in scope

—

DELETE policy is unchanged and a little odd: any authenticated user who isn't the item's owner can delete any claim row on that item, regardless of whether they placed it. Pre-existing behavior, not touched by this change, but worth a separate look.

—

Frontend doesn't surface this yet. This is a backend-only change — the app's own-items query never joins items_status today, so nothing in the UI currently shows a child-list owner the claim data they can now read. That's a follow-up in the Giftamizer frontend repo.
